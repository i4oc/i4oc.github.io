Initiative for Open Citations

The Initiative for Open Citations I4OC is a collaboration between scholarly publishers, researchers, and other interested parties to promote the unrestricted availability of scholarly citation data.

ANNOUNCEMENT

For the reasons explained below, I4OC has discontinued its List of Participating Publishers, and has introduced a new I4OC Star Publishers List, which includes the names of all publishers who meet our new open reference submission criteria, which are that the named publishers have submitted to Crossref the references within journal articles for least 75% of their current content (where current content exists) and for at least 25% of their back files (where back files exist).

About

Citations are the links that knit together our scientific and cultural knowledge. They are primary data that provide both provenance and an explanation for how we know facts. They allow us to attribute and credit scientific contributions, and they enable the evaluation of research and its impacts. In sum, citations are the most important vehicle for the discovery, dissemination, and evaluation of all scholarly knowledge.

As the number of scholarly publications is estimated to double every nine years, citations – and the computational systems that track them – enable researchers and the public to keep abreast of significant developments in any given field. For this to be possible, it is essential to have unrestricted access to comprehensive bibliographic and citation data in machine-readable form.

However, the present scholarly communication system inadequately exposes the knowledge networks that already exist within our literature. Citation data are not usually freely available to access, they are often subject to inconsistent, hard-to-parse licenses, and they are usually not machine-readable.

An initiative to open up citation data

The aim of the Initiative for Open Citations is to improve that situation, by promoting the availability of metadata describing scholarly citations that are structured, separate, and open.

Structured means the data representing each publication and each citation instance are expressed in common, machine-readable formats, and that these data can be accessed programmatically. Separate means the citation instances can be accessed and analyzed without the need to access the source bibliographic products (such as journal articles and books) in which the citations are created. Open means the data are freely accessible and reusable.

Key benefits of achieving this aim include:

  • The establishment of a global public web of linked scholarly citation data to enhance the discoverability of published content, both subscription access and open access. This will particularly benefit individuals who are not members of academic institutions that have subscriptions to commercial citation databases.

  • The ability to build new services over the open citation data, for the benefit of publishers, researchers, funding agencies, academic institutions and the general public, as well as to enhance existing services.

  • The creation of a public citation graph to explore connections between knowledge fields, and to follow the evolution of ideas and scholarly disciplines.

Relationship with other initiatives

I4OC is part of a broader movement for promoting openness of bibliographic metadata more generally. In particular, I4OC has a close relationship with the Initiative for Open Abstracts (I4OA), a sister initiative of I4OC aimed at promoting openness of abstracts of scholarly publications. I4OC and I4OA are managed by different teams, but these teams consist partly of the same individuals. While we hope that scholarly publishers will make a general commitment to openness of bibliographic metadata and will support both initiatives, it is possible for a publisher to support I4OC without supporting I4OA, or vice versa.

The historical availability of open references

Because many scholarly publishers use Crossref DOIs to identify their publications, I4OC initially chose to focus its attention on these publishers. When I4OC started in 2017, only a minority of these publishers submitted publication reference lists to Crossref along with their other publication metadata, and even fewer chose to make these references openly available. Subsequently, a growing number of publishers submitting references to Crossref chose that they should be open. As of May 2022, the fraction of publications with open references had grown from 1% to 89% of the ~60 million articles with references deposited with Crossref.

Following a policy change by the Crossref Board, from 3rd June 2022 the categories of 'closed' and 'limited' references were removed, and all references submitted to Crossref are now open, together with all other metadata relevant to each publication. This step allows submitted references within the Crossref members’ articles to be distributed without restriction through all of Crossref's Metadata Delivery services, including the REST API and bulk metadata dumps, to any interested party. However, at present (July 2022) most publications bearing Crossref DOIs do not have submitted references. In addition, many scholarly publications use DOIs supplied by other DOI registration agencies, and yet others do not use DOIs at all. Many reference lists are published as plain text within PDF documents. Thus the references from the majority of the world's scholarly publications are still not available in machine-readable form for third-party use. The work of I4OC has barely begun!

I4OC Star Publishers

Formerly, I4OC listed publishers who were making the references that they deposited with Crossref openly available. A publisher submitting any open references to Crossref qualified for inclusion in the I4OC Participating Publisher List, even if that publisher has submitted references for just a small fraction of its publications. However, since 3rd June 2022, all references submitted to Crossref are open by default, rendering the old I4OC Participating Publishers List meaningless as a means of discriminating between publishers, since all are now included. For this reason, use of that list has been discontinued and the list has been removed from the I4OC web page. The closed list (as of 2nd May 2022) is still available here [Add link to separate Figshare document] as a historical record.

Because the majority of Crossref members do not include references in the metadata they submit to Crossref, the emphasis of I4OC is now to encourage publishers to submit references for all their publications, both for current issues and for back files. I4OC has therefore initiated a new way to acknowledge those publishers that are submitting the majority of their references to Crossref, the I4OC Star Publishers List, presented below. This list is restricted to Crossref members that have submitted DOI metadata to Crossref (i.e. those that are or have been actively publishing entities bearing Crossref DOIs). For admission to the list, a publisher must meet our new open reference submission criteria, namely that the number of a publisher's articles with deposited references must exceed a certain percentage of all its articles with Crossref DOIs.

I4OC realizes that submission of references requires technical and time investment, and is therefore more complex than a simple policy change of opening those references that have already been submitted to Crossref, and that it may take extra time and effort to complete the submission of references for back files, once reference submission for current content has started. We also know that some journal items bearing Crossref DOIs do not have references, for example editorials, news items and book reviews. For these reasons, the thresholds for inclusion in the I4OC Star Publishers List have been set quite low: submission of references for at least 75% of that publisher's journal article current content (i.e. those journal articles published in the past three years)(where current content exists), and for at least 25% of its journal article back files (where back files exist).

We further realize that other forms of publication exist - editorials, book reviews, conference proceedings, book chapters, books, etc. - that may or may not include references, whereas journal articles should all have references. Because we wish the criteria for inclusion to be simple and unambiguous, we have limited them to references from journal articles. Nevertheless, we strongly encourage publishers to submit references for all their publications that include references.

The I4OC Star Publishers List

Following each publisher's name in the list, the percentages of journal articles with submitted references and the total number of journal articles in the current content and back files categories are given. The name used for each publisher is the name registered with and provided to I4OC by Crossref.

The format of the I4OC Star Publishers List is as follows:

Publisher namecurrent content: X% of A
back files: Y% of B

X% = percentage of current content journal article publications that have submitted references,

A = total number of current content journal article publications,

Y% = percentage of journal article back file publications that have submitted references, and

B = total number of journal article back file publications,

where X% must be > 75%,

where Y% must be > 25%,

where A + B must be > 0 for publisher name inclusion, and

where A must be > 0 for inclusion of current content data, and

where B must be > 0 for inclusion of back file data.

The I4OC Star Publishers List will be updated quarterly, and will include the name of any publisher whose Crossref-submitted references have increased to meet the inclusion criteria since the previous update.

Submission of references to Crossref

Submission of publication references to Crossref is possible for all Crossref members. Details are given in the I4OC FAQs page. If you have not yet registered to use Crossref DOIs, you will first need to become a Crossref member (see https://www.crossref.org/membership/). Further details are given in the I4OC FAQs.

Frequently asked questions

How do I join I4OC?

The Initiative for Open Citations (I4OC) is an advocacy organization that campaigns for open scholarly bibliographic citations. It has no formal membership, and no formal links with either Crossref or OpenCitations. Its present focus on Crossref references is simply because Crossref is the largest single repository of open scholarly references. Publishers who submit references to Crossref for the majority of their publications are commended by I4OC by being listed in the I4OC Star Publishers List.

How do I support I4OC?

If you are an scholarly organization, infrastructure or project that wishes to express support for the Initiative for Open Citations, or is interested in building on and promoting the availability of open citation data, please write to us at info@i4oc.org, and send us a copy of your logo with your permission to display it, which we will then do in the Stakeholders section of our web page.

I am a scholarly publisher already depositing references to Crossref. How do I publicly release them?

All references submitted to Crossref are, since 3rd June 2022, open by default.

Does the Initiative for Open Citations cover future citations only or also historical data?

The criteria for inclusion of a publisher's name in the I4OC Star Publishers List includes both current and future content and back files.

How do I check the status of my reference submission to Crossref?

You can check the present status of your reference submission to Crossref by entering your Crossref Member Name in the search box at https://www.crossref.org/members/prep/. In the Results, which show summary metadata statistics for the publisher, the first item “References” shows the percentage of your registered DOI publications for which you have already submitted references. If “References” shows 0%, this means that in fact you have not yet submitted any references to Crossref. Results for References and other types of publication metadata are given separately for Journal Articles, Books, and Book Chapters, and for Current Content, Back Files or All Time (i.e. Current Content and Back Files combined), selected by using the drop down menus at the top left and top right of the Results page. The total number of registered DOIs for your Books, Book Chapters and Journal Articles are listed above these menus.

If, in addition to submitting references to Crossref, you submit your publication Abstracts and other available metadata such as ORCiD author identifiers, you will be helping to further Open Science and will be supporting the Initiative for Open Abstracts (I4OA, https://i4oa.org), the sister organization of I4OC.

Is there a programmatic way to check the reference submission status of a publisher or a journal?

A convenient summary of any publisher’s or journal’s metadata is available using the Crossref REST API, with the results viewed by an appropriate tool, or using a JSON viewer in a Web browser.

The metadata held by Crossref for a publisher, including the reference submission status for current content and back files, may be obtained using the Crossref MemberID, e.g. for the Public Library of Science (PLoS): https://api.crossref.org/members/340.

Where the MemberID is unknown, it may be found by searching for the Publisher Name registered at Crossref (e.g. Public Library of Science (PLoS)) at https://www.crossref.org/members/prep/. and then looking at the right end of the resulting URL (e.g. https://www.crossref.org/members/prep/340). Similarly, the metadata held by Crossref for a journal, including the reference submission status for current content and back files, may be obtained using the journal ISSN, e.g. for the journal PLoS ONE: https://api.crossref.org/journals/1932-6203.

The ISSN for a journal may conveniently be found from the ISSN Portal or from Wikipedia, by searching on the journal name.

How do I submit references to Crossref for my publications?

To submit references to Crossref, you need to use Crossref DOIs for your publications. If you have not yet registered to use Crossref DOIs, you will first need to become a Crossref member (see https://www.crossref.org/membership/).

If you use DOIs from a different registration agency, please accept the apologies of I4OC for not listing you, but at present we can record only those publishers submitting open references to Crossref.

Information about how to add references to your existing Crossref DOI metadata records is given at https://www.crossref.org/documentation/metadata-stewardship/maintaining-your-metadata/add-references/.

Nevertheless, we strongly encourage publishers to submit references for all their publications that include references.

In case of difficulties with reference submission to Crossref, please contact support@crossref.org.

Publishers using the Open Journal Systems publishing software may automate the submission of references to Crossref by using the OJS-Crossref Reference Linking and Deposit Plugin. This plugin will automatically add article references to the other DOI-registered metadata held by Crossref. Crossref will then automatically check if any DOIs can be found for the publications identified by the submitted references. Further details are given by OJS here and from PKP’s Crossref OJS Manual, plus there's a very active PKP Community Forum which has more information on how to modify your OJS instance to submit metadata and register DOIs with Crossref. Alternatively you can contact the Support team at PKP, the organization responsible for the Open Journal System (OJS).

The OPTIMETA Project is developing a separate OJS plugin to achieve the same goal.

How do I access open citation data?

Once submitted, the references for individual scholarly publications may be accessed through the Crossref REST API. For information on how to use the interface, see their REST API guide. (Results from example query: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1038/227680a0.)

Open citations are also available from OpenCitations, an infrastructure created to house and preserve open scholarly bibliographic and citation metadata and to publish them in an interoperable standards-compliant machine-readable RDF format as Linked Open Data under the Creative Commons CC0 Public Domain Waiver. OpenCitations is progressively and systematically harvesting citation data from Crossref and from other sources. The principal OpenCitations database is COCI, the OpenCitations Index of Crossref Open DOI-to-DOI Citations, which contains in excess of 1.3 billion open citations.

Under what licensing terms is citation data being made available?

Crossref exposes article and reference metadata without a license, since it regards these as raw facts that cannot be licensed. The structured citation metadata within the OpenCitations Corpus are published under a CC0 Public Domain Waiver, to make explicit that these data are open and available for reuse for any purpose.

My journal is open access. Aren’t its articles’ citations automatically available?

No. Although Open Access articles may be open and freely available to read on the publisher’s website, their references are not separate, and are not necessarily structured or accessible programmatically. Additionally, although metadata for these publications may be submitted to Crossref, their references are not necessarily included. This is why it is important for each publisher using Crossref DOIs to ensure that the references within every one of its publications are submitted to Crossref.

What if I do not use Crossref DOIs?

I4OC is keen to see the references from all scholarly publications made openly available in centralized open repositories for programmatic access and reuse. However, for the limited purpose of creating the I4OC Star Publishers List, we at present record only those publishers submitting open references to Crossref.

If you use DOIs from a different registration agency, please accept the apologies of I4OC for not listing you.
